Amitriptyline Drug Profile

Generic Name

Amitriptyline

Description

A tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) that inhibits the reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin, with strong anticholinergic, antihistaminic, and antiadrenergic properties.

Indications

  • Major Depressive Disorder
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Migraine prophylaxis
  • Other mood-related disorders

Off-Label Uses

  • Insomnia
  • Nocturnal enuresis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic pain syndromes
  • Irritable bowel syndrome

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

ParameterDescription
AbsorptionWell absorbed orally; peak plasma levels in 3–6 hours; bioavailability ~30–60%
DistributionHigh volume of distribution (~14–17 L/kg); crosses BBB and placenta; ~95% protein bound
MetabolismExtensively metabolized in liver by CYP2D6, CYP2C19, and CYP3A4 to active metabolite nortriptyline
ExcretionPrimarily via urine (as metabolites); <2% excreted unchanged
Half-life10–28 hours (average ~21 hours); nortriptyline: 23–31 hours
Steady StateReached in approximately 5–7 days
Clearance0.5–1.5 L/min (hepatic)
Order of KineticsFirst-order kinetics

Interactions & Side Effects

Common Side Effects
  • Dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision
  • Weight gain, sedation, orthostatic hypotension
Serious Effects
  • QT prolongation, seizures, serotonin syndrome
Drug Interactions
  • SSRIs, MAOIs: Serotonin syndrome
  • CYP inhibitors: ↑ amitriptyline levels

Pregnancy Class

FDA Category C – use only if benefits justify risk

Common Brands in Pakistan

  • Amtil (Hilton Pharma)
  • Triptizol (GSK)
  • Amitone (Sami Pharmaceuticals)
  • Amyline (Bosch)

Recommended Dose

  • Depression: Start 25–50 mg at bedtime; Maintenance: 75–150 mg/day
  • Neuropathic Pain: 10–25 mg at bedtime, titrate up
  • Elderly: Start low (10–25 mg)
